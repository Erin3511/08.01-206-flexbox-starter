Expats Guide to Barcelona

Hi. I'm Erin.

I am a Responsive Designer & Developer Jack-of-all-trades, Mom, Wife, Adventure Seeker, & Forever Learner. Check out the sections below to catch a few of my tips on living your best life (with kids) in Barcelona, Spain.

How Gaudí!

Give yourself plenty of time to visit La Sagrada Familia, Casa Battló, Casa Milà (La Pedrera), and Park Güell. Any of these structures will be sure to amaze you and help you discover the simultaneous genius and madness of Antoni Guadí. Don't forget your camera with lots of disc space. You'll thank me later.

Beaches Galore

There are so many great beaches it will be hard to choose from. Playa de Bogatell, is the closest to city center that offers beautiful views and soft sand. Playas de El Prat de Llobregat beaches are great for families and KITESURFING! Costa Brava is must stop with several quaint fisherman towns to tour. Be sure to check out Tossa de Mar, and Cadaqués.

Family Fun

Our go-to family places are El Bosc de les Fades, is a hidden secret near the Wax museum off of La Rambla. The Magic Fountain ...and...IT'S FREE! CosmoCaixa, where kids can explore and participate in some hands-on science. Catalunya en Miniatura is great to see tourist sites from all over Spain in miniature form plus there is a kid's train and adventure park!
